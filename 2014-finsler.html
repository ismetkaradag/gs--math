Master Class on
Finsler Geometry and Applications

10-17 April 2014

Galatasaray University, İstanbul, Turkey

(in collaboration with GSU and TUBITAK)

Objectives




This is a one-week long research school with a focus on the following thematic areas: Finsler geometry, dynamics, hyperbolic geometry, projective geometry, systolic geometry, Teichmüller theory.
 There will be some opportunities for young researchers to deliver research talks and poster presentations. Schedule will be announced on March 2014.


Some funding may be available for qualified research students coming from the region.

Program

The title and the abstract of the courses will be added once they are determined. The following information can be partial.

Sarah Bray
The dynamics of non-strictly convex Hilbert geometries

Any open, properly convex domain $\Omega$ in $\R\P^n$ admits a Hilbert metric, compatible with a Finsler norm. Of particular interest are compact quotients of $\Omega$ by discrete subgroups of $\PGL(n+1,\R)$. The dynamical, topological, algebraic, and regularity properties of such quotients with the line flow have been exhaustively studied by Benoist and others in the case that $\Omega$ is strictly convex and consequently $\delta$-hyperbolic. My agenda is to expand previous studies to the convex but not strictly convex case for $\Omega$. In this talk, I will introduce a class of higher dimensional examples, constructed by Benoist, which exhibit some hyperbolicity properties. I will explicitly describe the topological and ergodic dynamical properties of the line flow on the projective triangle, which plays a focal role as an obstruction to hyperbolicity in the non-strictly convex examples of Benoist. I will assure you that we expect this obstruction to be surmountable.

Moon Duchin
Nilpotent groups and their sub-Finsler geometry

I will discuss the structure of finitely generated nilpotent groups and their large-scale geometry. I'll build up some of the Mal'cev theory for nilpotent groups and explain Pansu's theorems describing their asymptotic geometry in very concrete terms. One theme will be exploring how the geometry of the limit metric gives information about the discrete group.

Viveka Erlandsson
Margulis cusps of hyperbolic 4-manifolds

Consider a discrete subgroup G of the isometry group of hyperbolic n-space and a parabolic fixed point p. The Margulis region consists of all points in the space that are moved a small distance by an isometry in the stabilizer of p in G, and is kept precisely invariant under this stabilizer. In dimensions 2 and 3 the Margulis region is always a horoball, which gives the well-understood picture of the parabolic cusps in the quotient manifold. In higher dimensions, due to the existence of screw-translations (parabolic isometries with a rotational part), this is no longer true. When the screw-translation has an irrational rotation, the shape of the corresponding region depends on the continued fraction expansion of the irrational angle. In this talk we describe the asymptotic shape of the Margulis region in hyperbolic 4-space corresponding to an irrational screw-translation. As a consequence we show that the corresponding parabolic cusps are bi-Lipschitz rigid. As a corollary to the asymptotic shape we also obtain a necessary discreteness condition for isometry groups of hyperbolic 4-space, which can be viewed as a generalization of Shimizu's and Jorgensen's conditions in lower dimensions. This is joint work with Saeed Zakeri.

Ludovic Marquis
Coxeter groups in Hilbert Geometry

I will present a construction of convex bodies Omega of the real projective space which admit discrete subgroup of automorphism Gamma such that the quotient Omega/Gamma is compact, or of finite volume or convex-cocompact or geometrically finite. This construction is a given by a Theorem of Tits Vinberg and uses Coxeter group. After, i will discuss the geometrical properties of general quotient Omega/Gamma and moduli space of convex projective manifold (a.k.a such quotient Omega/Gamma).

Thomas Mettler
Compatibility of two-dimensional conformal and projective structures and applications to Finsler 2-spheres of constant curvature.

Recall that a projective structure P on a manifold M is an equivalence class of affine torsion-free connections on TM where two such connections are called equivalent if they share the same unparametrised geodesics. A conformal structure [g] on M is called compatible with a given projective structure P if P contains a connection preserving [g]. In this talk I will show that the space of conformal structures compatible with a projective structure P is given by the kernel of a projectively invariant quasi-linear first order differential operator. Furthermore, in the surface case, I will show that the conformal structures compatible with P are in one-to-one correspondence with certain holomorphic curves in a disk bundle over M. These results allow to rederive a Theorem of Bryant about rectilinear Finsler structures of constant curvature on the 2-sphere.

Hideki Miyachi
Rough homotheties on metric spaces

I would like to talk rough homotheties on metric spaces. It is known that if a Finsler space is not isometric to a Minkowski space, the Finsler space does not admit any k-homothety with k\ne 1. Motivated by this theorem, I would like to consider the existence problem of rough homotheties (rough similarites) on metric spaces. I will deal with the hyperbolic disk. If time permits, I will discuss the problem for Teichmuller space.

Constantin Vernicos
Introduction to Hilbert geometries

Hilbert geometries are defined in the interior of a convex set and are a generalisation of the Klein model (or projective model) of the Hyperbolic geometry. The aim of these lectures will be to introduce the audience to the main properties of these metric spaces and some of the current questions.

Sumio Yamada
Convex Bodies in the Euclidiean, Spherical, Hyperbolic and Teichmuller Spaces

The goal for the series of lectures is to convey the importance of convexity and to share the ideas used in understanding the geometric properties of convex bodies appearing in various contexts in mathematics. I will start with the Euclidean convex bodies, and its associated Finsler geometry, and then generalize the arguments to curved spaces, including the sphere, the hyperbolic space, and finally the Teichmuller space. Some knowledge of Riemannian geometry and Riemann surfaces would be helpful, but not necessary.
